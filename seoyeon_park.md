# Seo-Yeon Park

**Role:** Token-Gated Community Builder  
**Location:** Seoul, South Korea  
**Bio:** Former K-pop fan community moderator who launched an independent artist collective with on-chain membership on Base.

---

## Story: The Community She Actually Owned

Seo-Yeon had moderated fan communities for years. Tens of thousands of members. She built the culture, wrote the rules, resolved every conflict. Then the platform changed its algorithm. Engagement dropped 70% overnight. Then they introduced ads. Then they sold the data. She had built something real inside someone else's walls.

She minted 500 membership tokens on Base — 0.02 ETH each, one per person, non-transferable. Access to a private group, monthly live sessions with independent Korean artists, early access to releases. Holder list public on-chain. No platform could change who was in.

It sold out in five days. Mostly her existing community, people who trusted her more than any platform.

At the first live session, an artist performed unreleased tracks for 200 people on a video call. Seo-Yeon watched the chat scroll — members from Seoul, LA, Berlin, Jakarta, all there because they had chosen to be, not because an algorithm had shown them a notification.

Afterward, a member messaged her: *"This feels like it's actually ours."*

Seo-Yeon had never had a platform say that to her before.
