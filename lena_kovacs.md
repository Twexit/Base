# Lena Kovacs

**Role:** Freelance Translator  
**Location:** Budapest, Hungary  
**Bio:** Literary translator working with global clients, now receiving all payments via USDC on Base.

---

## Story: The Invoice That Arrived

For seven years, Lena chased invoices. A publisher in New York once took four months to pay. A startup in Singapore bounced a wire. A client in Brazil said the bank "lost" the transfer. She spent more time following up on money than translating.

Then a client offered to pay in USDC on Base. She was skeptical. She set up a wallet in an afternoon.

The payment arrived eleven seconds after the client clicked send.

She stared at the transaction hash on Basescan like it was a magic trick. She refreshed. Still there. She sent herself a test transaction of $1 just to feel it happen again.

Now she adds a wallet address to every invoice. About half her clients use it. The other half still wire. She can tell them apart immediately — one group pays in seconds, the other group pays in stories about their bank's processing times.

She's started charging a 3% "wire surcharge." Nobody has complained yet.
