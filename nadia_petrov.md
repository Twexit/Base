# Nadia Petrov

**Role:** Music Producer  
**Location:** Kyiv, Ukraine  
**Bio:** Electronic music producer funding her studio and supporting displaced artists through on-chain royalty splits on Base.

---

## Story: Split 11 Ways

The track took six weeks to finish. Eleven people contributed — vocalists, a violinist, a sound engineer who mixed from a basement in Kharkiv, a drummer recording on a laptop with a borrowed microphone. Coordinating payment through banks across three countries and a war zone was impossible.

Nadia set up a royalty split contract on Base. Every time someone bought the track or it earned streaming revenue that converted to USDC, the contract split it automatically — 11 wallets, proportional shares, no middleman, no delay.

The track earned $340 in the first month. Not a fortune. But the drummer in Kharkiv received $28.50 in his wallet within seconds of each payout. He sent Nadia a voice note: *"I heard the notification. I cried. I didn't think anyone would pay me for music again."*

The contract had no admin. Nobody could change the splits. Nobody could decide to delay or redirect a payment. The math just ran, quietly, every time someone listened.

Nadia started a second track the next day.
