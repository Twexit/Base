# Emmanuel Nwachukwu

**Role:** On-Chain Educator  
**Location:** Enugu, Nigeria  
**Bio:** High school computer science teacher building a free Solidity curriculum paid for by a Base grant.

---

## Story: The Class He Couldn't Stop Teaching

Emmanuel taught computer science to 16-year-olds who had never owned a laptop. The school had one computer lab with eleven machines, three of which worked reliably. He taught them HTML on Tuesday afternoons.

He applied for a Base ecosystem education grant on a whim. He wrote the application at midnight, submitted it, and forgot about it.

They approved $4,000.

He bought five Raspberry Pi kits. He built a 12-week Solidity curriculum. He translated it into Igbo for students who struggled with technical English. He found a local developer to guest-lecture for free every other week.

By week eight, a 17-year-old named Chisom had written a simple voting contract from scratch. She deployed it on Base testnet, checked Basescan, saw her contract address, and said — quietly, to nobody in particular — *"I made that."*

Emmanuel had been teaching for nine years. He had heard students say things like that before, usually about a drawing or an essay. He had never heard it said about something that lived on a blockchain.

He submitted the grant report on time. He was already writing the application for year two.
