# Omar Khalil

**Role:** Protocol Founder  
**Location:** Beirut, Lebanon  
**Bio:** Economics graduate who built a stablecoin savings protocol on Base after Lebanon's banking system collapsed.

---

## Story: What Banks Forgot to Be

Omar watched his father lose $60,000 in the Lebanese banking crisis. The money was there — on paper, in an account, provably his. The bank simply would not give it back. There was no recourse. There was no timeline. There was only waiting, and then eventually, acceptance.

Omar was 24. He studied economics. He understood, intellectually, what had happened. That didn't make it hurt less.

He moved to Cyprus for a year and learned Solidity. He came back to Beirut and built a savings protocol on Base: USDC deposits, no withdrawal locks, no bank holding the keys. Users held their own keys. The contract had no admin function that could freeze funds. He had an auditor verify this publicly.

He told no one it was inspired by his father.

The protocol grew slowly. Most users were Lebanese. Some were Argentinian. A few were Turkish. They shared one thing: they had all watched a bank decide that the money wasn't theirs anymore.

At 3,000 users, his father finally asked what he'd been building. Omar showed him the contract on Basescan. His father read it slowly. He didn't understand all of it.

*"Nobody can lock it?"* he asked.

*"Nobody,"* Omar said.

His father nodded and didn't say anything else for a while.
