# Isabella Ferreira

**Role:** Impact Verifier  
**Location:** Florianópolis, Brazil  
**Bio:** Environmental scientist using on-chain attestations on Base to verify carbon credit retirements for small reforestation projects.

---

## Story: The Tree That Had a Hash

The problem with carbon credits was that nobody could agree on what was real. Isabella had spent four years watching credits get sold twice, verification reports get buried, and satellite data get interpreted generously by people with financial incentives to be generous.

She built a simple attestation system on Base. Field surveyors uploaded GPS coordinates and photo hashes. A second verifier, independent, confirmed on-chain. The attestation was timestamped, immutable, publicly queryable. Not a cure for fraud — but a clear, permanent record of what was claimed, by whom, and when.

A small reforestation cooperative in Minas Gerais was her first client. Forty-seven farmers, 800 hectares, three years of growth. She issued 214 attestations. Every tree survey, every canopy measurement, every rainfall log — hashed and anchored to Base.

A European carbon buyer, skeptical of Brazilian credits after years of scandals, reviewed the chain of attestations before signing. They told her: *"We can see everything. We can see who verified and when. That's the first time we've been able to say that."*

They bought the credits.

Isabella did not think blockchain would save the rainforest. But she thought transparency might help. And transparency, at least, she could build.
