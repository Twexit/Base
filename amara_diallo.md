# Amara Diallo

**Role:** Micro-Lender  
**Location:** Conakry, Guinea  
**Bio:** Community organizer running an on-chain micro-lending pool for local market vendors on Base.

---

## Story: The Pool That Remembered

Banks in Conakry required collateral Amara's neighbors would never have. She had watched three small businesses fail not for lack of skill — but for lack of $200 at the right moment.

She read about on-chain lending pools. She spent three months learning. She raised $800 from the diaspora community — cousins in Paris, a friend in New York, a stranger on Discord who just believed in the idea.

The pool went live on Base. Loans of $50 to $150, repaid in USDC over 30 days. No bank. No forms. Wallet address as identity. Repayment history recorded on-chain, immutable.

The default rate in the first six months: zero.

Not because people couldn't fail. Because everyone knew everyone. The blockchain didn't create trust — it just held it. Aminata, who sold vegetables at the morning market, repaid three days early and borrowed again. She told Amara: *"This pool remembers that I pay. The bank never did."*

Amara wrote that sentence in her grant application. They got the funding.
